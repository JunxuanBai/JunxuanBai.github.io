Conferences for VR/AR/MR

IEEE VR Conference
Founded in 1993, IEEE VR conference is the premier international academic venue in the VR community. The conference receives papers that are submitted to TVCG.

IEEE International Symposium on Mixed and Augmented Reality (ISMAR)
ISMAR is founded in 2002, and it is a leading international academic conference in the field of augmented reality and mixed reality. The conference receives articles on graphics and VR. The best papers will be recommended to TVCG.

The ACM Symposium on Virtual Reality Software and Technology (VRST)
VRST is founded in 1994 and is organized by ACM. It is an international forum in the VR/AR field with a good reputation. In the past years, the best papers in the forum were recommended to journals such as TVCG.

Journals for VR/AR/MR

Virtual Reality & Intelligent Hardware (Elsevier)
Virtual Reality & Intelligent Hardware (VRIH) is an open-access journal owned by Elsevier. It is founded in 2019, which mainly focuses on VR and its intelligent hardware. The journal offers high-quality single-blind peer review and is a bi-monthly journal.

Virtual Reality (Springer)
The VR journal is an international publication under Springer, founded in 1995, focusing on VR/AR/MR. The journal accepts state of the art reviews. The impact factor of 2020 is 5.095, the submission to first notification takes 125 days, and the submission to acceptance takes 353 days.

Replicability of CG papers

Graphics Replicability Stamp Initiative
Graphics Replicability Stamp Initiative (GRSI) is a graphics paper reproducibility certification website formed by a group of volunteers, mainly focusing on TOG, TVCG, and CGF. If the paper can be reproduced or has provided source code, it will be certified by the website.

Papers with Code
The mission of "Papers with Code" is to share the code, dataset, evaluations of Machine Learning papers.

Ceres Solver is an open source C++ library for modeling and solving large, complicated optimization problems.

Kiwisolver is an efficient C++ implementation of the Cassowary constraint solving algorithm.
